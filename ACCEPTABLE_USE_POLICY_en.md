## 1. Design Philosophy

This tool is an analytical instrument for observing human behavioral tendencies and personality traits as coordinates in a neutral space — beyond the binary of "good" and "evil." Every data point plotted here represents nothing more than a variation in adaptive strategy. No coordinate implies superiority or inferiority, worth or worthlessness.

Users are strongly encouraged to approach this tool with the kind of non-judgmental, observational mindset found in mindfulness practice or Zen philosophy — the ability to see things as they are, without layering personal emotion onto what you observe. That said, this is a recommendation, not a requirement. You are an adult. I trust you to figure it out.

## 2. Permitted Uses

- Personal introspection, self-understanding, and behavioral self-debugging
- Objective structural analysis of historical figures, literary works, or political ideologies
- Academic research and education in psychology, behavioral ecology, and organizational theory
- Rigorous comparative studies in peer-reviewed or institutional academic contexts

## 3. Prohibited Uses

The following uses are explicitly prohibited. Confirmed violations will result in the immediate revocation of your license to use or distribute this tool.

### 3-1. Weaponizing the Tool Against Others

Plotting a third party without their consent and using the results to cause psychological distress. Also prohibited: using the tool to attach value judgments — such as "inferior" or "harmful" — to individuals based on their coordinate position.

### 3-2. Creating or Spreading Discriminatory Content

Creating, publishing, or distributing data that denigrates specific individuals, groups, races, or human attributes by associating them with "unpleasant" or "harmful" creatures — insects, pests, or otherwise.

### 3-3. Non-Consensual Profiling

Producing detailed plots of third parties without their knowledge or consent, and sharing or publishing that information.

## 4. A Note on Non-Human Entities in Plots

If you choose to include non-human organisms in your plots, please limit them to scientifically or behaviorally grounded classifications (e.g., "solitary apex predator," "collective defense organism"). Placing creatures commonly regarded as disgusting or harmful in the same coordinate space as specific human attributes — in a way that clearly implies a derogatory comparison — falls squarely under Section 3-2.
